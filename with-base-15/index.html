FizzBuzz in Base 15

In base 10 you can spot multiples of 2 and 5 from the last digit alone, because 2 and 5 divide the base. Pick a better base: 3, 5, and 15 all divide 15, so in base 15 the entire FizzBuzz decision lives in the final digit. Divisible by 15 means it ends in 0; by 3 means it ends in 0, 3, 6, 9, or C; by 5 means it ends in 0, 5, or A. The branch collapses into a fifteen-entry lookup table — the modulo is hiding in the base conversion.
