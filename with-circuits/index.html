The circuit — every wire painted from a real evaluation of the netlist

green wire = logic 1, faint wire = logic 0. Diagram rendered once from the yosys netlist by netlistsvg; the signal values are recomputed live, gate by gate, from the same netlist JSON.

The circuit is one 4-bit adder feeding two divisibility detectors. The whole trick: 16 ≡ 1 (mod 3) and (mod 5), so for n = 16·hi + lo both residues of n equal the residue of hi + lo. The adder folds the byte to a 5-bit sum (0–30), and each detector is a small cloud of gates over just those five wires. The 8 number LEDs carry the input, gated off whenever fizz or buzz fires — so the LEDs spell the whole answer, just like the printed version.

Source: fizzbuzz.v → yosys → 41 AND, 32 OR, 9 XOR, 10 NOT. test.js checks the synthesized netlist against arithmetic on all 256 possible inputs.
